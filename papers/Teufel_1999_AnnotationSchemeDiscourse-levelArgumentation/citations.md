# Citations

## Key Citations for Follow-up

- John Swales. 1990. *Genre Analysis: English in Academic and Research Settings.* Cambridge University Press. Used as the main source for the notion of recurring and obligatory communicative moves in research articles, which this paper adapts into a sentence-level annotation scheme. *(p.2)*
- William C. Mann and Sandra A. Thompson. 1987. "Rhetorical structure theory: description and construction of text structures." Used as the main contrast case: this paper argues for local argumentative role labels instead of full RST-style discourse trees over complete documents. *(pp.1-2)*
- Joost G. Kircz. 1991. "The rhetorical structure of scientific articles: the case for argumentative analysis in information retrieval." Cited as prior work on argumentative analysis of scientific articles and as evidence that research articles have field-specific rhetorical regularities. *(pp.1-2)*
- Julian Kupiec, Jan O. Pedersen, and Francine Chen. 1995. "A trainable document summarizer." Representative extractive summarization baseline used to motivate why better discourse-aware training resources are needed. *(p.1)*
- Daniel Marcu. 1997. "From discourse structures to text summaries." Another discourse-based summarization approach used as contrast for the paper's lighter-weight, locally annotated alternative. *(p.1)*
- Geoff Thompson and Yiyun Ye. 1991. "Evaluation in the reporting verbs used in academic papers." Supports the idea that local sentence cues can expose evaluative stance toward prior work. *(p.2)*
