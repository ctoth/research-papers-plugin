Jonnalagadda, Goyal, and Huffman review the literature on automating the data-extraction step of systematic reviews, focusing on methods that identify or extract concrete review data elements from abstracts or full-text articles. Across 26 included studies, they find promising results for narrow participant, intervention, and outcome extraction tasks, often with reported F-scores above 0.7, but they also show that the literature is fragmented across incompatible schemas, private corpora, and limited task coverage. The main conclusion is that no unified extraction framework yet covered the full systematic-review abstraction process, even though several component tasks were already tractable. *(pp.1, 4-14)*
