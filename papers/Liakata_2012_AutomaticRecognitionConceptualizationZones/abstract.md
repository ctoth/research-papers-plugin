The paper introduces Core Scientific Concepts, an 11-category sentence-level discourse annotation scheme for full scientific articles, and studies how well those categories can be recognized automatically in chemistry and biochemistry papers. The authors compare support vector machines, LibLinear classifiers, and conditional random fields using lexical, grammatical, and document-structure features, finding that frequent categories such as Experiment and Background are recoverable with useful accuracy while rare categories remain difficult. They also argue that the resulting annotations are useful for discourse-aware summarization and for information extraction pipelines that need to distinguish hypotheses, methods, observations, results, and conclusions. *(p.991)*
