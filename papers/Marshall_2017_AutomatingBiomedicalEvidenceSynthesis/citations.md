## Key Citations for Follow-up

- Kiritchenko, de Bruijn, Carini, Martin, and Sim (2010), *ExaCT*. RR treats this as the main full-text precursor for extracting trial characteristics, and the contrast with ExaCT explains why RR emphasizes PDFs and data scarcity. *(p.10)*
- Marshall, Kuiper, and Wallace (2014), *Automating risk of bias assessment for clinical trials*. This is the direct predecessor for RR's bias-classification component. *(p.8)*
- Marshall, Kuiper, and Wallace (2016), *RobotReviewer: Evaluation of a System for Automatically Assessing Bias in Clinical Trials*. This is the most direct follow-up if you want the fuller evaluation of the RoB subsystem rather than the short ACL demo summary. *(pp.8, 12)*
- Wallace, Kuiper, Sharma, Zhu, and Marshall (2016), *Extracting PICO Sentences from Clinical Trial Reports using Supervised Distant Supervision*. This is the core method paper behind RR's PICO sentence extraction. *(p.10)*
- Zhang, Marshall, and Wallace (2016), *Rationale-Augmented Convolutional Neural Networks for Text Classification*. RR's improved RoB classifier is built around this rationale-aware document model. *(p.8)*
