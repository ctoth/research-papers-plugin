# Citations

## Key Citations for Follow-up

- Kenneth F. Schulz, David A. Grimes. 2002. "Generation of allocation sequences in randomised trials: chance, not choice." Cited as part of the empirical and methodological literature that sharpened the randomization and allocation-concealment reporting items in CONSORT 2010. *(p.1, p.6)*
- Kenneth F. Schulz, David A. Grimes. 2002. "Allocation concealment in randomised trials: defending against deciphering." Used as methodological support for demanding a concrete concealment mechanism instead of vague assurances. *(p.5, p.6)*
- David Moher, Kenneth F. Schulz, Douglas G. Altman. 2001. "The CONSORT statement: revised recommendations for improving the quality of reports of parallel-group randomized trials." The immediately previous checklist version that CONSORT 2010 updates. *(p.1, p.6)*
- Douglas G. Altman, Kenneth F. Schulz, David Moher, et al. 2001. "The revised CONSORT statement for reporting randomized trials: explanation and elaboration." Companion interpretive document that the authors repeatedly recommend using together with the checklist itself. *(pp.3-4, p.6)*
- Sally Hopewell, Mike Clarke, David Moher, et al. 2008. "CONSORT for reporting randomised controlled trials in journal and conference abstracts." Provides the structured-abstract guidance referenced by new checklist item 1b. *(p.5, p.6)*
- An-Wen Chan, Asbjorn Hrobjartsson, M. T. Haahr, Peter C. Gotzsche, Douglas G. Altman. 2004. "Empirical evidence for selective reporting of outcomes in randomized trials." Supports the new requirement to disclose outcome changes after trial commencement. *(p.5, p.7)*
- An-Wen Chan, Jesper P. A. Ioannidis, Douglas G. Altman. 2004. "Empirical evidence for selective reporting of outcomes in randomized trials: comparison of protocols to published articles." Another direct motivation for explicit outcome-change and protocol-reporting requirements. *(p.5, p.6)*
- Joel Lexchin, Lisa A. Bero, Benjaman Djulbegovic, Otavio Clark. 2003. "Pharmaceutical industry sponsorship and research outcome and quality: systematic review." Used as part of the rationale for explicit funding disclosure in item 25. *(p.5, p.7)*
