# Citations

## Key Citations For Follow-up

- David Moher, Kenneth F. Schulz, Douglas G. Altman, and the CONSORT Group. 2001. "The CONSORT statement: revised recommendations for improving the quality of reports of parallel-group randomized trials." Used as a direct reporting-guideline precedent that helped shape the STROBE initiative's process and checklist framing. *(p.5)*
- David Moher, Douglas G. Altman, Kenneth F. Schulz, and colleagues. 2000. "Opportunities and challenges for improving the quality of reporting clinical research: CONSORT and beyond." Cited as part of the broader reporting-guideline movement that STROBE extends from trials to observational studies. *(p.5)*
- Patrick M. M. Bossuyt, Johannes B. Reitsma, David E. Bruns, et al. 2003. "The STARD statement for reporting studies of diagnostic accuracy." Included as a neighboring reporting-guideline effort and precedent for domain-specific checklist development. *(p.5)*
- Julian P. T. Higgins, Douglas G. Altman, Peter C. Gøtzsche, et al. 2007. "The Cochrane Collaboration's tool for assessing risk of bias in randomised trials." Referenced in the discussion distinguishing transparent reporting from direct methodological-quality scoring. *(p.5)*
- Joni C. J. Little, Douglas G. Altman, Matthias Egger, et al. 2007. "Strengthening the Reporting of Genetic Association Studies (STREGA): an extension of the STROBE initiative." Cited as a domain extension for genetic-association studies built on top of STROBE. *(pp.3, 5)*
- Des Jarlais, Lyles, Crepaz, and the TREND Group. 2004. "Improving the reporting quality of nonrandomized evaluations of behavioral and public health interventions: the TREND statement." Another closely related checklist effort for nonrandomized studies and public-health interventions. *(p.5)*
- Jan P. Vandenbroucke, Erik von Elm, Douglas G. Altman, et al. 2007. "Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology (STROBE): explanation and elaboration." The companion operational document that explains the reasoning behind each checklist item and provides examples. *(pp.1, 3, 5)*
